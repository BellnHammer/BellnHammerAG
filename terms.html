Project Terms of Service

1. Scope of Work

Any modifications, additions, or reductions to the original scope must be requested in writing and may be subject to additional costs and timeline adjustments.

2. Payment Terms

  • A deposit is required before work begins
  • Progress invoices will be issued at the completion of each project phase and are due within 7 business days
  • Final payment is due upon substantial completion
  • Late payments will incur a service charge of 8% per month or the maximum rate allowed by law

3. Deposits & Advance Payments

Before physical work begins: 50% of the deposit is non-refundable to cover administrative and procurement costs.

After physical work has started: 100% of the deposit is non-refundable. All deposits are applied toward the final project price.

4. Change Orders

Any deviation from the agreed-upon scope must be documented in a formal Change Order signed by both parties before additional work begins.

5. Project Timeline

Work is scheduled to commence on an agreed-upon date with an estimated completion timeline. Timelines are subject to delays caused by weather, supply chain issues, hidden conditions, or permitting.

6. Materials & Site Conditions

The client typically provides materials unless otherwise specified in the estimate. If the contractor sources materials, additional costs for procurement and delivery will apply. The job site must be accessible and free of obstructions.

7. Minimum Labor Requirement

A minimum labor charge of two (2) hours will be applied to all handyman and small-scale service tasks, regardless of the actual time required to complete the work.

8. Material & Finish Selections

All material and finish selections are considered final upon client approval or purchase. Changes after approval require a formal change order, and the client assumes all additional costs.

Finish Standards: Work meets industry standards for residential construction with uniform color when viewed under normal lighting conditions from a distance of six (6) feet. Older homes may retain imperfections, variations in texture, or minor inconsistencies inherent to the existing structure.

9. Workmanship & Warranty

All work is performed professionally and includes a limited 24-month warranty from the completion date. This warranty excludes damages caused by misuse, neglect, or external factors beyond our control.

10. Permits & Client Responsibilities

The contractor will obtain all necessary permits and coordinate inspections. The client is responsible for providing any required documentation in a timely manner.

11. Liability & Insurance

Bell & Hammer maintains general liability and workers' compensation insurance. We are not liable for pre-existing conditions or issues arising from client-supplied materials.

12. Project Cancellation

Before work begins: The deposit is refundable minus any planning costs already incurred.

After work has begun: No deposit is refundable. The client must pay for all labor, materials, and subcontractor costs incurred to date.

13. Unforeseen Conditions

The contractor is not responsible for hidden or unforeseen conditions not reasonably visible at the time of the estimate, including but not limited to rot, mold, asbestos, lead paint, water intrusion, and pest damage.

14. Client-Supplied Materials

The contractor does not warranty or assume responsibility for the quality, suitability, or performance of client-supplied materials.

15. Access & Utilities

The client must provide reasonable access to the work area, including parking, electricity, water, and restroom facilities where applicable.

16. Client Communication & Site Conduct

All project communication should go through the designated project manager. Interference or "hovering" that causes delays to the crew may result in additional labor charges. The client is responsible for restricting pet and child access to the work area.
