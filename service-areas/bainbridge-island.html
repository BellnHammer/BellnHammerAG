About Bainbridge Island

Island Standards, Delivered

Bainbridge Island occupies a unique position in Kitsap County. A 35-minute ferry ride from downtown Seattle, it attracts homeowners who want space, trees, and quiet — but who are accustomed to a certain level of quality and professionalism. They've seen what good design looks like. They know what they want. And they've been burned before by contractors who couldn't deliver on the island's particular demands.

Working on Bainbridge means understanding the logistics. Materials come across on the ferry or via barge. Scheduling matters more because you can't just run to the supply house in 20 minutes. We plan our projects on Bainbridge with extra precision — full material lists ordered in advance, staged deliveries, and crews who arrive ready to work without wasted trips. That kind of planning is something every project deserves, but on the island, it's not optional.

The housing on Bainbridge reflects the community's character. Winslow, the walkable town center near the ferry terminal, has a mix of historic homes, newer infill construction, and properties being renovated to meet modern standards while respecting neighborhood character. Rolling Bay and Battle Point have larger lots with mid-century homes from the 1950s through 1970s — many with original cedar siding, shake roofs, and floor plans that made sense for a different era. These homes have great bones and incredible settings, but they need updating: open-concept kitchens, primary suite additions, new windows that capture those water views properly.

Lynwood Center and Island Center, toward the south end, tend toward more rural properties — some with acreage, some with guest cottages that need renovation, and newer custom builds tucked into the trees. The custom finish work on Bainbridge is where we particularly excel: detailed trim carpentry, built-in cabinetry, tile work that's genuinely artful, and the kind of precision that discerning homeowners notice and appreciate.

Bell & Hammer is licensed, bonded & insured and pulls permits through the City of Bainbridge Island for all work on the island. We use galvanized fasteners and marine-grade hardware on exterior projects exposed to salt air. Our client portal keeps you updated in real time — especially valuable when you're commuting to Seattle and can't check on the job site every day. We treat your island home with the same respect we'd want for our own, because in Kitsap County, reputation is everything.

FAQ

Bainbridge Island Questions

Do you charge extra for working on Bainbridge Island?+

We don't add an "island surcharge." Our estimates account for ferry and material logistics transparently. What you see in the estimate is what you pay. We plan our Bainbridge projects efficiently to minimize unnecessary trips and keep costs fair.

Can you do a whole-home remodel while I'm commuting to Seattle?+

Absolutely — many of our Bainbridge clients work in Seattle during the week. Our client portal gives you real-time updates, photos, and progress notes so you always know what's happening. We also do a weekly walkthrough summary so nothing catches you off guard.

Do Bainbridge permits take longer than the mainland?+

The City of Bainbridge Island has its own permitting department and design review process, which can add time — especially for projects in historic areas or near the shoreline. We factor this into every project timeline and submit thorough applications to keep reviews moving.

What kind of finish work do you do?+

Custom trim, built-in shelving and cabinetry, wainscoting, tile installation, hardwood flooring, and detailed carpentry. The finish work is where the personality of a remodel comes through, and we take pride in getting it right. No shortcuts, no "good enough."
